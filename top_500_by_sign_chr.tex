Pearson's Chi-squared test of independence\\

$\chi^{2} = 44.6$, $df = 23$, $p-value = 0.0044$\\

The most strongly positively and negatively {\textit {COMT}}\den correlated genes are distributed proportionately unequally across the 22 autosomes and the X chromosome. There are significantly ($p = 0.00034$) fewer negatively correlated than positively correlated genes on Chromosome 1 than would be expected from the overall proportion of negatively {\textit {COMT}}\den correlated genes across the 500 most strongly correlated genes. The {\textit {COMT}} gene is located on Chromosome 22.\\
